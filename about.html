About the Project

SYLLABUS is a
student decision tool
for KIU.

Before you pick a course, you deserve to know what you're getting into. SYLLABUS gives every KIU student one place to read honest course summaries, browse past exams and materials, and see what students before them actually thought — so you can choose with information, not guesswork.

What is this course actually about? → Read the syllabus summary
What did past exams look like? → Browse student-uploaded materials
Is this course right for me? → Read reviews from peers who took it
The Problem

How students chose courses
before SYLLABUS

💬

WhatsApp groups

Scattered across dozens of group chats. Advice is ephemeral — a message from two semesters ago is buried under thousands of others and impossible to find when you need it.

🎲

Word of mouth

You ask a friend. They ask their friend. The answer depends on who you happen to know. Students with bigger networks get better information — everyone else guesses.

📋

The official portal

Course codes and credit counts. No syllabus summary, no student reviews, no past exams. Technically accurate, completely useless for deciding whether a course is right for you.

The Solution

Everything a student needs
before they enroll

01

Syllabus Summaries

Every course page contains a plain-language summary of what the course covers — topics, structure, and what the professor expects. Not bureaucratic text. A real summary written to be understood.

02

Past Exams & Materials

Students upload past midterms, homework sets, exercises, and notes directly to the course page. Shared knowledge compounds over time.

03

Honest Student Reviews

After taking a course, students leave a review and a 1–5 star rating. Real answers from real people who sat through it.

04

Professor Context

The same course taught by a different professor is a different experience. Each Course + Professor pairing has its own page, its own rating, its own reviews.

05

Semester-by-Semester History

SYLLABUS tracks each offering separately. See how the same course was rated under one professor two years ago versus a different professor last semester.

06

Works for Mandatory Courses Too

You cannot always choose what you take — but you can still prepare. Knowing what past students experienced helps you plan your semester, study strategy, and expectations before day one.

How It Works

From question to decision

  1. 1

    Search for your course

    Type the course name or browse by degree and semester. Every offering is listed separately by professor.

  2. 2

    Click the course poster or title

    You go directly to the course page — syllabus summary, professor background, offering history, and student-uploaded materials like past exams and exercise sheets.

  3. 3

    Read student reviews

    Scroll through what other KIU students wrote after taking this exact course under this exact professor.

  4. 4

    Make an informed decision

    You now know what the course covers, how demanding it is, and what students like you thought. Choose with confidence.

  5. 5

    Give back after your semester

    Leave a review and upload anything useful — homework sheets, exam papers, your notes. The next student will find it.
