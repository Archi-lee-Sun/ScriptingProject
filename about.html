About the Project

SYLLABUS is a
student decision tool
for KIU.

Before you pick a course, you deserve to know what you're getting into. SYLLABUS gives every KIU student one place to read honest course summaries, browse past exams and materials, and see what students before them actually thought — so you can choose with information, not guesswork.

The Problem

How students chose courses
before SYLLABUS

💬

WhatsApp groups

Scattered across dozens of group chats. Advice is ephemeral — a message from two semesters ago is buried under thousands of others and impossible to find when you need it.

🎲

Word of mouth

You ask a friend. They ask their friend. The answer depends on who you happen to know. Students with bigger networks get better information — everyone else guesses.

📋

The official portal

Course codes and credit counts. No syllabus summary, no student reviews, no past exams. Technically accurate, completely useless for deciding whether a course is right for you.

The Solution

Everything a student needs
before they enroll

01

Syllabus Summaries

Every course page contains a plain-language summary of what the course covers — what topics, what structure, what the professor expects from students. Not the official bureaucratic text. A real summary written to be understood.

02

Past Exams & Materials

Students upload past midterms, homework sets, exercises, and notes directly to the course page. If someone sat through this course two years ago and saved the final exam — you can find it here. Shared knowledge compounds over time.

03

Honest Student Reviews

After taking a course, students leave a review and a 1–5 star rating. What was the workload really like? Was the grading fair? What do you wish you knew before starting? Real answers from real people who sat through it.

04

Professor Context

The same course taught by a different professor is a different experience. SYLLABUS keeps them separate — each Course + Professor pairing has its own page, its own rating, its own reviews. You see who is teaching this semester and find every course they have taught before.

05

Semester-by-Semester History

Courses run every semester. SYLLABUS tracks each offering separately. See how the same course was rated under one professor in Semester III two years ago versus a different professor last semester. History teaches you what to expect.

06

Works for Mandatory Courses Too

You cannot always choose what you take. But you can still prepare. Even if a course is required for your degree, knowing what past students experienced helps you plan your semester, your study strategy, and your expectations before day one.

How It Works

From question to decision

  1. 1

    Search for your course

    Type the course name or browse by degree and semester. You will see every offering of that course — each professor who has taught it listed separately.

  2. 2

    Read the course page

    The course page shows you a syllabus summary, the professor's background, a history of how this course was rated in past semesters, and student-uploaded materials like past exams and exercise sheets.

  3. 3

    Read student reviews

    Scroll through what other KIU students wrote after taking this exact course under this exact professor. Real experiences, not marketing copy.

  4. 4

    Make an informed decision

    You now know what the course covers, how demanding it is, what past exams looked like, and what students like you thought. Choose with confidence — or prepare better for what's mandatory.

  5. 5

    Give back after your semester

    Once you finish the course, leave a review and upload anything useful you saved — homework sheets, exam papers, your notes. The next student will find it when they need it most.
