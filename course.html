About this course

This course takes you from zero to fully functional relational databases. You start with conceptual design — learning to model the real world using Entity-Relationship diagrams — then work through the entire pipeline: mapping ER models into database schemas, writing SQL DDL to implement them, and applying normalization rules to ensure clean, non-redundant designs. A heavy portion of the course is devoted to SQL querying, starting from basics and progressing to complex joins, CTEs, and window functions. The final weeks cover physical data organization and index structures so you understand not just what queries return, but how fast they do it.

Workload is substantial — 60 contact hours plus 120 hours of self-study are expected. Prof. Knirsch runs a tight ship: participation grade rewards students who engage in lectures, complete individual Learning Journals, and deliver lab tasks on schedule. Industry orientation is central to her teaching philosophy; expect real-world datasets and problems from the start.

Prof. Knirsch brings 30+ years of real industry experience into every lecture — she doesn't just teach you SQL, she teaches you to think like a database engineer.
