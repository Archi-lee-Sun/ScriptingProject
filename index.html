EMK

Eva Maria Knirsch

Computer Science — Databases & Data Analytics

Eva Maria Knirsch holds master's degrees in Computer Science and English, including an MA from the State University of New York as a DAAD scholar. With over 30 years of IT experience, she has served as Project Manager at the Robert Koch Institute, Senior Engineer at SRA International, and General Manager of the Information Security Forum. Since 2011, she has been an Adjunct Professor at HTW Berlin, specializing in databases and data analytics. Her teaching emphasizes practical, industry-ready skills designed to meet the high demands of the modern IT business world.

GN

Giorgi Nadareishvili

Computer Science — Theory & Foundations

Giorgi Nadareishvili earned his PhD from the University of Göttingen in Germany, where he later continued as a postdoctoral researcher. He has also held a postdoctoral position at Tbilisi State University. His scientific research is primarily focused on the field of non-commutative topology, bridging abstract mathematics with theoretical computer science. At KIU, he teaches foundational theory courses that explore the mathematical limits of computation — covering Turing machines, Gödel's incompleteness theorems, and the P vs NP problem.

Courses taught at KIU
  • Intro to Theory of Computation
WT

Walter Tichy

Computer Science — Software Engineering

Walter Tichy was a Professor of Software Engineering at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT) from 1986 to 2021, where he also served as Dean of Computer Science (2002–2004). He previously held positions as a Senior Scientist at Carnegie Group Inc. and spent six years on the faculty at Purdue University. A graduate of Carnegie Mellon University with both an MS and a PhD, his research focuses on software engineering, parallelism, and language technologies. Beyond academia, he is the co-founder of ParTec and ESCde and a member of ACM, GI, and IEEE.
